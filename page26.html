USA Roll Past France, Wins 5th Straight U17 World Cup Title

By Fiba.basketball published September 07, 2018

SANTA FE (FIBA U17 Basketball World Cup) â€“ The U.S. remained the dominant kings of the FIBA U17 Basketball World Cup, clinching their fifth straight title with a resounding 95-52 win over France in the 2018 Final.


Isaiah Stewart scored four double-figure US players with 15 points to go with 9 rebounds, while De'Vion Harmon scored 14 points, Romeo Weems scored 12 points, 9 rebounds, and Jalen Green scored 11 points.


The United States stretched its winning streak to 37 games by locking up their fifth consecutive U17 title, dating back to the 2010 inaugural event.


Elsewhere, France has already achieved its best-ever success by reaching the Final and can go home happy with a second-placed finish.


Killian Hayes was France's top 10-point scorer in the game, while Maxime Carene recorded 9 points and 4 blocks. But all that wasn't enough, particularly because Hayes ' big three, Malcolm Cazalon and Theo Maledon combined in the game for only 24 points-23.1 points below their average.


In the early stages, the American defense stifled the French and established a 12-7 lead that was extended to 19-13 by the men of Don Showalter on Green's three-pointer. After ten minutes, the difference was 23-14.


France kept fighting and cut the gap to 25-20 in the first half with six minutes left. But the Americans buckled down defensively to open a 45-22 lead on a 20-2 rally. Just before the half-time buzzer, Hayes knocked down a three-pointer and at intermission it was 45-25.


There was no advantage in making in-roads into the U.S. in the third quarter as France could not get any spots on defense first off and the Americans remained determined not to allow any actions to come easy-predominantly on the famed trio of France. After three quarters, the difference was 77-38.
