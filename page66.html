DENR Employee Shot Dead by Riding-in-Tandem Suspects in Antipolo

By Rappler.com posted June 08, 2018

An employee of the Department of Environment and Natural Resources was shot dead by unknown suspects on Friday morning, 8 June.
According to Antipolo City Police, 54-year-old Dominador Lucas was on his way to his office in Taytay, Rizal, when an unknown suspect shot him in his face and body. He died at once.
After the shooting, two unknown suspects fled to Sumulong Highway aboard a pink Yamaha Mio with no plate number. The motorcycle, the police said, was waiting at the Taktak Bridge.
Police said the Criminal Operations Team Scene was needed for a technical investigation.
Rappler recently reported that an average of 4 people are killed every day in the Philippines by riding-in-tandem shooters.
The Philippine National Police began listing statistics in October 2017, when police were taken out of the Duterte administration's campaign against illegal drugs.
