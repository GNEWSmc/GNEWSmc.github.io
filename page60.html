1.4 million Filipino Families Fell victim to Common Crimes in Q3 – SWS

By Michael Bueza posted December 01, 2018

Philippines – According to a Social Weather Stations (SWS) survey released on Thursday, November 29, about 1.4 million families were victims of common crimes in the third quarter of 2018.
From September 15-23, the SWS survey showed that 6.1 percent of Filipino families (around 1.4 million families) registered victimization of any of the common crimes over the past six months.
This is higher than in June 2018, when the casualties were 5.3 percent or about 1.2 million people. But this rate is the same as the September 2017 6.1 percent registered.
Common crimes relate to personal property pickpocket or theft, break-ins, carnapping, and physical violence.
The latest survey also found that 5.6% of Filipino families (about 1.3 million people) fell victim to property crimes–higher than 5.1% in June 2018, but still below 6.1% in March 2018.
Meanwhile, 0.7% or about 159,000 families reported that their members had been injured by physical violence, up from a record low of 0.2% in June.
Fear of crime, the presence of drug addicts ' hardly changed '
In addition, the survey found that the family's fear of burglary and unsafe streets and the presence of drug addicts had hardly changed since the last quarter.
Approximately 52% of Filipino adults said they agreed with the statement, "In this neighborhood, people are usually afraid that robbers might break into their homes."
