Terrifying Moment the Electric Transformer Explodes as a 6.9 Magnitude Earthquake Hits the Philippines

By Isabella Nikolic posted December 15, 2019

Footage taken during a 6.9 magnitude earthquake in the Philippines today shows the moment when an electrical transformer exploded while tremors shook the country.
Three people have been confirmed dead in Padada, but according to local media, the full list of casualties is unknown.
A child died in a village in Davao del Sur's town of Matanao when the wall of her house tumbled down as the ground shook and hit her in the head, officials said.
The footage was recorded in Davao on the southern island of Mindanao, north of the epicenter of the quake.
A bright flash of light zips across the overhead cables before a loud explosion is heard.
Citizens on the streets start crying and fleeing for cover-up.
The United States Geological Survey confirmed that the earthquake struck South Cotabato at 2:11 p.m. local time.
Officials have confirmed that there have been a number of aftershocks across the country.
The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology reported that the magnitude of 6.9 quake had struck an area approximately 3.7 miles (six kilometers) northwest of Padada City in Davao del Sur Province. It had a range of 30 kilometers (18 miles).
The provincial police chief announced that the number of injured in the hardest hit areas had reached 62, with one confirmed fatality after a child had been trapped under a collapsed wall.
Commander Alberto Lupaz said there appeared to be some people trapped in the damaged market house, but the rescue efforts had been delayed. ' They (the rescuers) are trying to check the debris ... The aftershocks were too heavy, ' Lupaz said.
President Rodrigo Duterte, who is from Davao, was caught in the earthquake but was unharmed, officials said.
' The First Lady is ... Said that the car she was riding (in) was swaying, "said Salvador Panelo. ' They are not injured. ' Patients have been evacuated from two hospitals in Digos City and Bansalan City, Davao del Sur, according to local reports.
