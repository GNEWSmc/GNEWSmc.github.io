Typhoon Tisoy exits landmass via Occidental Mindoro

By Acor Arceo posted December 03, 2019

Typhoon Tisoy (Kammuri) is already 110 kilometers north-west of San Jose, Occidental Mindoro, or 135 kilometers north of Coron, Palawan.
Typhoon Tisoy (Kammuri) left landmass on Tuesday afternoon, December 3, from Occidental Mindoro, after landing four times in the Philippines.
The Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) said Tisoy is 110 kilometers northwest of San Jose, Occidental Mindoro, or 135 kilometers north of Coron, Palawan, in a newsletter released last Tuesday at 5 p.m.
At a relatively fast 25 kilometers per hour (km / h) it still flies west.
Tisoy continues to weaken as well, although it remains in the group of typhoons. It now has average winds of 130 km / h from the previous 140 km / h, but from the previous 195 km / h, its taste has slightly increased to 200 km / h.
These are the places where Tisoy made landfall:
• Gubat, Sorsogon - 11 pm on Monday, December 2
• San Pascual, Burias Island, Masbate - 4 am on Tuesday, December 3
• Torrijos, Marinduque - 8:30 am on Tuesday, December 3
• Naujan, Oriental Mindoro - 12:30 pm on Tuesday, December 3
