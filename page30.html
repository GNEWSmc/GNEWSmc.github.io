Baguio: Rape Cases Up, Minors Involved in Street Crimes

By Frank Cimatu posted December 04, 2017

Crime in Baguio has gone down considerably, with the exception of rape. The Baguio City Police Office (BCPO) announced that a little less than 3,000 crimes (2,966) were committed from January to October of this year.


That's almost half the number of crimes in the same time last year–5,857.

Rape, however, increased by 5 cases in the first 3 quarters of the year, or by 15 per cent from 2016, Police Superintendent Ramil Saculles said at the recent 4th Quarter City meeting of the Human Rights, Justice, Peace and Orders Council (CHRJPOC).


The police recorded the highest incidence of rape between April and July, coinciding with the beginning and end of classes, respectively. Most of the victims were between 15 and 18 years of age, with the youngest being 3 years of age.

Most were attacked between 10 p.m. and 4 a.m., and the suspects were mostly under the influence of alcohol.


In line with this, the façade of the Baguio City Hall has been turned orange since November 25 (and until December 12) in support of the "18 Days to End Violence Against Women" movement.


Saculles said that the index of crimes or street crimes decreased by 70 per cent from January to October, from 2,159 last year to just 645 this year.


He, however, said that there were 83 minors involved in theft and robbery, usually shoplifting and pickpocketing, and 41 minors who committed physical injuries.
